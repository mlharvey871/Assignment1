Ideal Job

I am interested in numerous aspects of IT and I would enjoy a career in either web design, software development or technical support. With my background in education, I believe that an ideal job (see Figure 3) for me would be as a Digital Content Designer/Builder (Seek, 2021). This position would require me to design an education-based web site and then be responsible for creating (and maintaining) a related Learning Management System (LMS). The position would require me to create a complete digital system that enables students to enrol in a specific course, access study materials and perform assessment tasks. I would need to ensure that the web site and LMS are both visually appealing and easy to navigate. The position would require me to work with course engineers and other stakeholders to ensure that all design requirements are met (see Figure 4).






Digital Content Designers need to understand how users interact with content and make sure that it meets user needs (Newson, 2019). This position appeals to me not only because of my background in education, but also because of my desire to solve problems and utilise my creative skills. I enjoy the challenge of being given a design brief and ensuring that my eventual design meets the needs of the specific users. I believe that this position would allow me to be creative with my designs, as well as being able to provide support to the people that eventually use the programs. I would also have the opportunity to continue working on the project even after it is launched, with a high likelihood of the prospect of being required to continue designing updates and improvements to the LMS. A good LMS should make incremental updates and keep up with industry trends (eLogic Learning, 2017).

The position (see Figure 5) requires the applicant to have experience designing and developing multimedia learning-products (Seek, 2021). The applicant would be required to demonstrate their ability to learning platforms either through a portfolio of previous designs or recognised qualifications. The position requires the potential applicant to have experience with graphic design and web site development, which will likely include a comprehensive knowledge of programming languages. They will also need to have the ability to use a variety of design tools and applications. The position requires strong User Interface (UI) skills and the ability to follow a design brief using numerous UI design tools. UI design tools allow the developer to develop a User Interface based on the design brief (Pedamkar, 2020). Perhaps the most essential requirement for the position is a passion for great design and the ability to be creative.




I currently possess only a limited understanding of programming languages and web design tools. I have a passion for design and the ability to follow set guidelines. In my previous positions, I have also demonstrated the ability to work as part of a team and collaborate with others. It is likely that a position of this magnitude is currently out of my reach, therefore I would need to develop a plan to obtain the skills, qualifications, and experience that the potential employer requires. I believe that a Bachelor of Information Technology (with electives in Web Design) would be a great starting point. I would then need to undertake further studies in web design and computer programming. My options may include postgraduate study, a Diploma of Web Design or even completing numerous online programming courses. The next step would undoubtedly be compiling a portfolio of my designs to demonstrate my skills and abilities, and conceivably learning to use UI design tools such as Adobe XD (see Figure 6).
