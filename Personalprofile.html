Personal Profile

Online tests:

1. Myers-Briggs Type Indicator Test (16Personalities, 2021)
2. Learning Styles Test (PersonalityMax, 2021)
3. Multiple Intelligences Test (PersonalityMax, 2021)

What do these results mean?

The results of the Myers-Briggs Type Indicator Test (MBTI) determined that my personality type is referred to as an ‘Advocate’. This test determined that I am introverted, assertive and an intuitive thinker. My unique personality traits make me complex but also quite versatile (16Personalities, 2021). The results determined that I confident in myself, passionate about my interests and ideas, and diplomatic when dealing with conflict. Overall, I see these results as supporting my belief that I enjoy being by myself, but I also possess the caring empathetic nature required to spend time with other people.

The results of the Learning Styles Test determined that I am predominantly a kinaesthetic learner, however the balance is very close between the three learning styles (kinaesthetic, visual, and auditory). The results state that I prefer ‘hands-on’ experiences, however I am equally capable learning through listening, viewing videos and reading texts. The Multiple Intelligences test is based on Howard Gardner’s Theory of Multiple Intelligences that states that we all excel with different types of intelligences (PersonalityMax, 2021). The results of this test were also quite balanced; however, my top three intelligences were intrapersonal (learn independently and through reflection), logical (use logic and reason) and kinaesthetic intelligence (learn through movement and experimentation). I believe that the three tests clearly show that I can learn (and interact with others) in different ways, however my natural tendency is to work alone.

What influence do these results have on teamwork and collaboration?

The results of all three tests determined that I have an introverted personality and generally try to avoid group work. I have the ability to be a leader, however I often avoid group-based situations. The tests also determined that ‘learning new skills’ is important to me, and it is my opinion that my desire to succeed will ensure that I push myself to work in groups and collaborate with other students (even if I am not comfortable with these situations). My respectful and diplomatic nature will be well suited to group projects as I possess the ability to listen to the ideas and opinions presented by other people, as well as the ability to share responsibilities.

What do the results suggest should be considered when forming a team?

When forming a team, I need to consider both my strengths and weaknesses. I need to understand that group-work and collaboration are an important part of the IT industry, and there will be times when I need to complete tasks that make me feel slightly uncomfortable. It will be important for me to get to know my fellow group members as soon as possible to alleviate any anxiety. I will need to ensure that I demonstrate the strengths of my ‘Advocate’ personality, such as my passion, creativity, and insightful nature (16Personailities, 2021). Conversely, I also need to be aware of my weaknesses (and attempt to improve them) such as my sensitivity to criticism and perfectionism.
